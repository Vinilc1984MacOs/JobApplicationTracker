💼 Job Application Tracker
A sleek, native macOS application built with SwiftUI to help you manage, track, and analyze your job hunting journey. Keep your pipeline organized, sync your interviews automatically, and gain insights into your target salary and application velocity—all stored securely and locally on your Mac.
✨ Features
	•	📊 Smart Dashboard: Instantly visualize your pipeline, success rates, average target salaries, and application momentum with beautiful, interactive charts.
	•	🗺 Geographic Mapping: Automatically geocodes your application locations and plots them on an interactive map bubble chart.
	•	🗓 iCal Interview Sync: Paste your private Google Calendar iCal link to automatically pull in and organize your upcoming interviews. Automatically extracts meeting links (Zoom, Meet, Teams).
	•	📄 Document Management: Store and categorize your resumes, cover letters, and portfolios, and link them directly to specific job applications.
	•	⚙️ Highly Customizable Engine: Use Regex rules to auto-categorize roles and seniorities. Customize your own status pipelines, work models, and UI colors.
	•	📥 Bulk Smart Import: Easily paste rows directly from Excel or Google Sheets to bulk-import your existing tracking spreadsheets.
🚀 Installation
You can download the compiled .dmg installer directly from the Releases page.
	1	Navigate to the Releases page.
	2	Download the latest JobTracker.dmg file.
	3	Double-click the .dmg file to open it.
	4	Drag the Job Application Tracker app into your Applications folder.
	5	Launch the app from Spotlight or your Launchpad!
🛠 Building from Source
If you prefer to compile the app yourself:
	1	Clone this repository: git clone https://github.com/yourusername/JobApplicationTracker.git
	2	Open the project in Xcode.
	3	Select your Mac as the build target.
	4	Press Cmd + R to build and run, or go to Product > Archive to create your own build.
🔒 Privacy & Security
Privacy First: Job Application Tracker does not use cloud databases. All of your job data, documents, and profile information are stored strictly locally on your machine in the macOS sandbox.
Designed with SwiftUI for macOS.
